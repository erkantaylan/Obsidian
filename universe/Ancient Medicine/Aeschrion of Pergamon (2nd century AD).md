# Aeschrion of Pergamon (2nd century AD)

Ancient Greek physician from Pergamon (modern-day Bergama, Turkey). One of [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]]'s early tutors and a member of the **Empirici** medical sect.

## Life

- Practiced medicine at the [[Asclepion of Pergamum]]
- Had great knowledge of **pharmacy and materia medica**
- Taught Galen during his four years of intensive medical education alongside other physicians like Stratonicus and Satyrus

## Notable Work

- Invented a celebrated (though superstitious) remedy for the **bite of a mad dog**, whose key ingredient was powdered crayfish
- The crayfish had to be caught when the sun and moon were in a specific alignment, then baked alive
- This remedy was mentioned with approval by both [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]] and Oribasius

## Connection to Galen

Aeschrion was part of a group of physicians who shaped Galen's early medical thinking. While Galen would go on to far surpass his teachers, the empirical and pharmacological grounding he received from Aeschrion influenced his later work.
