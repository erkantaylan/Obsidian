# Ibn Sina (Avicenna) (980 - 1037)

Persian polymath and physician, known in the West as **Avicenna**. Often called the **"Prince of Physicians"**, he wrote the most influential medical textbook in history.

## Life

- Born in **Afshana, near Bukhara** (present-day Uzbekistan)
- Child prodigy — summoned to treat the Sultan of Bukhara while still young
- After curing the Sultan, gained access to the royal library, which fueled his scholarship
- Wrote over **450 works**, around 240 surviving today
- Worked across medicine, philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, and chemistry

## Key Contributions

- Wrote ***The Canon of Medicine*** (Al-Qanun fi al-Tibb, c. 1025) — a massive medical encyclopedia that synthesized Greek, Roman, and Persian medical knowledge with his own clinical observations
- First to describe **meningitis**
- Recognized the **contagious nature of tuberculosis**
- Identified that diseases could spread through **water and soil**
- Explored the **connection between psychology and health**
- Made major contributions to anatomy, gynecology, and pediatrics

## The Canon of Medicine

- Synthesized the traditions of [[Hippocrates (c. 460–370 BC)|Hippocrates]] and [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]] into a single comprehensive work
- Used as the **standard medical textbook in European universities** until the 17th century
- Central to medical education at institutions like Padua and Bologna
- Set the standards for medicine across the Islamic world and Europe for over 600 years
