# Hippocrates (c. 460 - c. 370 BC)

Ancient Greek physician from the island of **Kos**, widely regarded as the **"Father of Medicine"**. He fundamentally changed medicine by separating it from superstition and religion.

## Life

- Born on the island of Kos, Greece
- Son of Heraclides, also a physician
- Learned medicine from his father and grandfather
- Studied other subjects with Democritus and Gorgias
- Traveled and practiced throughout Thessaly, Thrace, and the Sea of Marmara
- Died in Larissa, aged approximately 83–90

## Key Contributions

- **Separated medicine from religion** — rejected the idea that gods caused all disease, replacing it with rational observation
- Developed the **four humors** theory (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile) — later adopted and expanded by [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]]
- Pioneered **clinical observation and prognosis** as medical practice
- Introduced a **holistic approach**: body, mind, and spirit as interconnected
- Set foundations for modern surgery, urology, neurology, and orthopedics
- Established the **Hippocratic Oath**, still referenced in medical ethics today

## Legacy

- His work directly shaped [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]]'s medical philosophy, especially the humoral theory
- [[Ibn Sina (980–1037)|Ibn Sina]] later synthesized both Hippocratic and Galenic traditions in *The Canon of Medicine*
- Considered the starting point of **Western clinical medicine**
