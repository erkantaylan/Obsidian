# Marcus Aurelius (121 - 180)

Roman Emperor from 161 to 180 AD and **Stoic philosopher**. The last of the **Five Good Emperors** and the last emperor of the **Pax Romana**.

## Life

- Born **26 April 121** in Rome
- Raised by his mother and paternal grandfather after his father died when he was three
- Trained extensively in **Stoic philosophy** under Quintus Junius Rusticus
- Became emperor reluctantly — saw it as duty, not privilege
- Died **17 March 180** while on campaign

## Philosophy

- Author of ***Meditations***, one of the most important surviving works of **Stoic philosophy**
- Wrote it as a private journal, never intended for publication
- Themes: self-restraint, duty, respect for others, impermanence, rational thought
- Earned the title **"Philosopher King"** within his own lifetime

## Connection to Galen

- [[Galen (129–216)|Galen]] served as his **personal physician**
- Called Galen *"first among doctors and unique among philosophers"*
- Their relationship placed Galen at the center of Roman imperial life and gave him resources and prestige to continue his medical work
