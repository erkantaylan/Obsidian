# Galen (129 - c. 216)

Ancient Greek physician, surgeon, and philosopher born in **Pergamum** (modern-day Bergama, Turkey). One of the most influential figures in the history of medicine.

## Life

- Son of Aelius Nicon, a wealthy architect
- Began studying medicine at age 16 in Pergamum, then Smyrna (Izmir) and Alexandria
- Served as **chief physician to gladiators** in Pergamum (157 CE), gaining hands-on experience with trauma and anatomy
- Moved to Rome, eventually becoming personal physician to several Roman emperors

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that **arteries carry blood, not air** — overturning 400 years of accepted teaching
- Identified seven pairs of **cranial nerves**
- Described the **heart valves** and structural differences between arteries and veins
- Proved through experiments that the **brain controls the voice**
- Advanced pharmacology, surgery, and therapeutic methods
- Built on the **four humors** theory (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile)

## Legacy

- Wrote approximately **300 works**, around 150 surviving to this day
- His theories **dominated Western medicine for ~1,400 years**
- Arab physicians translated and preserved his works from the 9th century onward
- Eventually corrected during the Renaissance by Andreas Vesalius (1543) and later William Harvey
